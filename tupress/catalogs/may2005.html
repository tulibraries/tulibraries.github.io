May 2005

  • 1: Rich Westcott was inducted in the 21st Ward (Philadelphia) Sports Hall of Fame for his journalism work, his participation with the Phillies organization, and his 17 books on local and national sports.

  • 5-7: Gerda Lerner, author of Fireweed: A Political Autobiography, will be the keynote speaker at the American Council of Learned Societies at the Sheraton Society Hill Hotel in Philadelphia, PA.

  • 6: Bob Bloss, author of Rookies of the Year will be interviewed on KFRC 610-AM on Marty Lurie's Right Off The Bat, an Oakland A's pre-game show, at 2:30pm Pacific Time.

  • 7: Huping Ling has been invited by the Coalition of Asian Pacific Americans in St. Louis to give a presentation on her book, Chinese St. Louis: From Enclave to Cultural Community, at 7pm at the International Institute of Metro St. Louis at 3654 South Grand Blvd (Grand & Gravois).

  • 8: Jon Nese will be interviewed by Pennsylvania Cable Network about The Philadelphia Area Weather Book. Nese's interview is scheduled to air on May 8th at 9pm on PCN.

  • 9: Luisa Margolies will be interviewed about her book, My Mother's Hip on The Mark Bentley Show on KRSN (Los Alamos, NM) 10:10am Eastern time.

  • 9-10: Luisa Margolies, author of My Mother's Hip: Lessons from the World of Eldercare, will be running a session for seniors and health care providers at the 24th Annual Broward Aging Network Conference (BANC), Coconut Creek, FL.

  • 11-15: American Geriatrics Society Conference, Orlando, FL.

  • 12: Susan Korman, author of P Is for Philadelphia, will be signing books at Barnes and Noble in Lancaster, PA at 6:30pm.

  • 14: Richard Orodenker, editor of The Phillies Reader, will be co-hosting Doug Stirling's Sports Show on WCOJ-AM 1420 (West Chester, PA) from 3-5pm.

  • 15: Why I Burned My Book and Other Essays on Disability will be promoted as a giveaway on KPFA (94.1 FM in San Francisco) on the program "Pushing Limits," which will air at 6:30pm Pacific time.

  • 17: Bob Bloss, author of Rookies of the Year, will be signing books at The Trover Shop (221 Pennsylvania Ave. SE, Washington DC; tel: 202.537.BOOK) from 12:30-1:30pm.

  • 17-20: World Forum on Early Care and Education, Montreal, Quebec, Canada. Betty Bardige, author of At a Loss for Words: How America Is Failing Our Children and What We Can Do about It, will be presenting. The book will be on display in their bookstore.

  • 23: Luisa Margolies will be talking about My Mother's Hip with Laura Olson on KLGR (Minneapolis, MN) and Alan Palmer on WKCT (Bowling Green, KY).

  • 23: Richard Orodenker is scheduled to appear on "Daily News Live" on Comcast Sportsnet in Philadelphia at 5:30 pm to discuss The Phillies Reader.

  • 24: Luisa Margolies will be talking about My Mother's Hip with Rick Peters on WKSN (Jamestown, NY).

  • 26: Bob Bloss will be on Daily News Live on Comcast Sportsnet in Philadelphia at 5:30pm to discuss Rookies of the Year. Bloss will also be interviewed that day for Comcast Sportnet Mid-Atlantic (Bethesda, MD) for broadcast at a later time.

  • 26: Luisa Margolies will be talking about My Mother's Hip with Tron Simpson on KCMN (Colorado Springs, CO).

  • 26-29: American Literature Association Conference, Boston, MA.

  • 28-6 June: Congress of the Social Sciences and Humanities, University of Western Ontario.
