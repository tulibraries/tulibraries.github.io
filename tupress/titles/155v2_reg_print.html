Black Worker

The Era of the National Labor Union

edited by Philip S. Foner and Ronald L. Lewis

cloth EAN: 978-0-87722-137-1 (ISBN: 0-87722-137-5)
$39.95, Feb 89, Out of Print
416 pp


This volume covers landmark events in labor and race relations such as the strikes of Black rice harvesters, stevedores, and railroad workers; a joint strike of Black and white oyster shuckers; the constitution and prospectus of the National Colored Labor Convention; the Klu Klux Klan's efforts to terrorize Black laborers; the emigration of Black laborers from the South; and much more.


About the Author(s)

Philip S. Foner is Professor of History at Lincoln University, Pennsylvania. He is the author of History of the Labor Movement in the United States (4 volumes) and Life and Writings of Frederick Douglass (5 volumes).

Ronald L. Lewis is Assistant Professor in the Black American Studies Department at the University of Delaware. His most recent publication is Coal, Iron, and Slaves: Industrial Slavery in Maryland and Virginia, 1715-1865.
