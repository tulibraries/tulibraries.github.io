Black Worker

The Black Worker from the Founding of the CIO to the AFL-CIO Merger

edited by Philip S. Foner and Ronald L. Lewis

cloth EAN: 978-0-87722-197-5 (ISBN: 0-87722-197-9)
$39.95, Nov 80, Out of Print
640 pp


In addition to massive documentation of the relationship between blacks and the CIO and AFL, this volume includes sections on the Southern Tenant Farmers' Union, the Steel Workers' Organizing Committee, the National Negro Congress, tobacco workers and seamen, the Missouri Roadside Demonstration of 1939, blacks and the war economy, the March on Washington Movement, the Fair Employment Practices Committee, discrimination at West Coast shipyards, the Philadelphia "Hate Strike" of 1944, the National Negro Labor Council, and Paul Robeson.


About the Author(s)

Philip S. Foner is Professor of History at Lincoln University, Pennsylvania. He is the author of History of the Labor Movement in the United States (4 volumes) and Life and Writings of Frederick Douglass (5 volumes).

Ronald L. Lewis is Assistant Professor in the Black American Studies Department at the University of Delaware. His most recent publication is Coal, Iron, and Slaves: Industrial Slavery in Maryland and Virginia, 1715-1865.
